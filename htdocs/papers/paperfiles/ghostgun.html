Any mandate on ghost guns won’t stop them from being produced

    Ghost guns are unregulated weapons with no serial numbers that are made at home and can be bought by almost anyone (“What Are Ghost Guns?” ). In the article titled “The national mandate on ghost guns is a start. But it doesn’t go far enough” the writer, Illinois Senator Jacqueline Collins talks about how she helped lead Senate efforts to approve new legislation that would target ghost guns on the streets of Illinois. The proposed law requires any person who possesses or comes into possession of a gun or assembly part without a serial number to attain serialization. While this legislation may help with law-abiding citizens, many of the individuals creating/selling ghost guns are criminals who don’t care about a mandate to get their homemade gun serialized. 

The action is a step in the right direction, however, I believe that no matter the legislature, criminals will always find a way around the system. It can help try to prevent these guns from going untraced. However, 2,500 ghost guns were connected to federal criminal cases from April 2010 to April 2020 (“Ghost Guns.”). 30% of the guns recovered in California by the bureau of alcohol tobacco firearms and explosives (ATF) had no serial number on them, making them untraceable (“Ghost Guns.”). Collins means well with the proposed legislation, however, realistically the laws don’t “apply” to criminals. If a criminal knows that the law is to get their ghost gun serialized, does that mean they will do it? Of course not! A criminal isn’t going to get their gun serialized and then commit crimes with the same weapon. It would be a different level of self-incrimination on the criminal’s behalf. It’s much easier for a criminal to just “say” they serialized their weapon when they didn’t, or maybe not even mention at all that they have a weapon.

The legislation proposed by Collins is a good step in the right direction. It tries to address the main problem that ghost guns have; however, I feel that the law she helped push through will only apply to the law-abiding citizen; not the underground criminal who is committing crimes already and might not have much to lose. With legislation, Ghost Guns can be serialized and safely monitored when a law-abiding citizen purchases one. For a criminal, however, that may be a different story and they will continue to produce and distribute this weapons to use them in deadly crimes.
