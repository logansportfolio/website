The State of The Union and Joe Biden’s Remarks

The current president of the United States, Joseph R. Biden, recently gave his State of the Union Address for 2022. He talks about many topics including COVID-19 numbers, accessibility to prescriptions, childcare, and more. This paper will focus on three main topics President Biden covered: the Russian aggression against Ukraine; how to curve against inflation during a pandemic and a war; and finally, his call for Congress to pass the “Freedom to Vote Act,” also known as the “Jon Lewis Act.” President Biden was very firm in his statements, and I believe with President Biden by our side, we may be looking at a good future ahead of us.

 President Biden refers to Russian President Vladimir Putin’s attack on the country of Ukraine as “premeditated and totally unprovoked” (Biden 2) and this is completely true, as Ukraine has wanted to be an independent country ever since it gained independence in August of 1991 (“History of Ukraine”). Ukraine has done nothing to provoke Russia to attack or invade their country. Biden continues to say that Putin didn’t expect anyone to come to the aid of Ukraine, “But Putin was wrong. We are ready. We are united. And that’s what we did: We stayed united” (Biden 2). He states, “Putin may circle Kyiv with tanks, but he’ll never gain the hearts and souls of the Ukrainian people” (Biden 6). He says later in his speech, “The [free] world is holding him accountable, along with 27 members of the European Union'' (Biden 3). These are strong impactful words that I believe we can all agree help unite and bring us together even more. He’s completely right, the United States has given over $650 million dollars to Ukraine in the past year alone in funds and supplies (Shivaram). “Putin is now isolated from the world more than he has ever been” (Biden 3). Even though we are sending funds to Ukraine, we aren’t engaging directly with the Russian forces. Biden states, “But let me be clear: Our forces are not engaged and will not engage in the conflict with Russian forces in Ukraine” (Biden 4). I believe the best reason Biden is not directly sending troops to Ukraine is since Putin has said, on multiple occasions, that he would use nuclear force if the United States were to get “physically” involved in the conflict (Sanger).

With the current conflict in Russia and the current sanctions being put upon them, many of the world's countries' economies are paying the toll. In the United States, the average gas prices are $4.17 per gallon, which is an extra 64 cents per gallon since the Russian invasion of Ukraine (Blanco). Biden attempts to calm down the population, he says, “I’m taking robust action to make sure the pain of our sanctions is targeted at the Russian economy and that we use every tool at our disposal to protect American businesses and consumers” (Biden 5). He announced the release of over 60 million barrels of oil from reserves around the globe with the help of 30 other countries (Biden 5). He says, “These steps will help blunt gas prices here at home… But I want you to know: We’re going to be okay. We’re going to be okay” (Biden 5). Biden’s words “We’re going to be okay” is a way to help try and comfort the American people who are dealing with the rising inflation. The release of oil reserves over time, hopefully, will reduce gas prices if Biden’s plan works the way he thinks it will. He says that’s why one of the first things as President he did, passed the American Rescue Plan. “We needed to act and we did… the American Rescue Plan helped working people and left no one behind” (Biden 6). He goes in-depth on the growth of the economy since he has taken office, with the economy growing at a rate of 5.7% last year (Biden 7). Biden makes the point that for the past 40 years, tax breaks were supposed to be for those at the top and a “trickle-down” effect was supposed to happen, benefiting everyone else. “But that trickle-down theory led to a weaker economic growth, lower wages, bigger deficits, and a widening gap between the top and everyone else…” (Biden 7). Biden makes a huge comment that shook the room stating, “I think I have a better idea to fight inflation: Lower your costs, not your wages.” and goes on further to say, “Instead of relying on foreign supply chains, let’s make it in America” (Biden 11). I believe we can do this by bringing more businesses in who are willing to keep costs low for change to happen. Some companies like Walmart are promising to keep costs low to try and help curve inflation (Monica).

    Biden goes into prescription drug costs and mentions a boy named Joshua. Biden states, “Insulin costs $10 a vial to make… But drug companies charge families like Joshua and his dad up to 30 times that amount” (Biden 12). He then tells the story about Joshua and states:

I spoke with Joshua’s mom. Image what it’s like to look at your child who needs insulin to stay healthy and have no idea how in God’s name you’re going to be able to pay for it - what it does to your family, but what it does to your dignity, your ability to look your child in the eye, to be the parent you expect yourself to be. I really mean it. Think about that. That’s what I think about. You know, yesterday - Joshua is here tonight, but yesterday was his birthday. Happy Birthday, buddy, by the way (Biden 12).

This story is very impactful and gives the listeners an actual look into someone's personal life who has to deal with these conditions and these prices. That alone helps Americans see the actual effects it's having on other human beings just like them. If drug companies were regulated and held to a certain price standard, Americans could save money on medications they need such as Josh’s insulin. Drug companies should be capped at a certain amount for how much they can charge for medication as these medications aren’t a choice for some people and they rely on them to stay alive (Meller).

    Biden later in his speech starts talking about the Jon Lewis Act also known as the Freedom to Vote Act. He says, “In state after state, new laws have been passed not only to suppress the vote… but to subvert the entire election. We can’t let this happen” (Biden 22). He goes on to say “Tonight, I call on the Senate to pass the Freedom to Vote act. Pass the John Lewis Act - Voting Right. And while you’re at it, pass the DISCLOSE Act so Americans know who is funding our elections” (Biden 22). The Freedom to Vote act addresses voter registration and voting access, integrity, security, and campaign financing (“S.2747 - 117th Congress (2021-2022): Freedom to Vote Act.”). It will help expand access to voting, and limit removing voters from voting rolls, making it easier to vote. The bill also wants to establish Election Day as a federal holiday and most importantly wants to make anyone able to vote, even certain convicted felons (as long as you aren't currently serving your sentence) (“S.2747”). This bill is very important due to the recent drama that has arisen during the past election and the numerous claims of election fraud. People shouldn’t be denied the right to vote, even if they have made mistakes in their past.

    President Biden’s State of the Union Address was well formulated and was very impactful on the country. It not only addressed the issues that we have been working on over time but also issues we need to work on fixing in the future. Biden has a way of stating these plans with a sense of care and unity, making sure that we are reminded we are all in this together and not alone. Furthermore, Biden’s claims and words are impactful, accurate, and create a sense of belonging and togetherness. Biden is setting up a road for success for the country and trying to make the best during the pandemic, sovereign war, and inflation. I believe that with Biden on our side, we can ride through the rest of this pandemic and this conflict, and make the nation even better than it was previously.
